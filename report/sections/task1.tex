\section{Task 1: Transfer Learning for Pet Classification}

In this task, we investigate fine-grained image classification on the Oxford-IIIT Pet Dataset using deep neural networks. The primary objective is to study the effectiveness of transfer learning by fine-tuning ImageNet-pretrained models on a relatively small-scale pet breed classification dataset. A pretrained ResNet-34 model is adopted as the baseline architecture, where the original classification head is replaced to match the 37 target categories.

Based on the baseline model, we further explore the influence of data augmentation strategies, optimization settings, and training hyperparameters, including optimizer type, learning rate configuration, batch size, weight decay, and training epochs. A multi-stage hyperparameter search is conducted to obtain the final training configuration.

To evaluate the contribution of pretrained representations, an ablation study is performed by comparing pretrained initialization with random initialization under different optimization settings. In addition, attention-based architectures are investigated for comparison, including SE-enhanced ResNet-34 and a lightweight Vision Transformer model.

Model performance is analyzed using validation and test accuracy together with training dynamics, confusion matrix analysis, representative error cases, and DeepDream visualizations, providing further interpretation of the learned visual representations and remaining classification challenges.
