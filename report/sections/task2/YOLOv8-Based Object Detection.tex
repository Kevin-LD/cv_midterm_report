\subsection{YOLOv8-Based Object Detection}

Two YOLOv8 configurations are trained and evaluated in this work: YOLOv8n with an input resolution of $640 \times 640$, and YOLOv8s with an input resolution of $768 \times 768$.

The first configuration, YOLOv8n-$640$, is designed as a lightweight baseline to verify the correctness of the training pipeline and provide a reference point for subsequent comparison. The second configuration, YOLOv8s-$768$, is designed as a higher-capacity setting under the hardware constraints of the experimental platform (\texttt{NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060 Laptop GPU}, 8GB VRAM). Compared with the baseline, the second configuration jointly scales both the network capacity and the input image resolution in order to improve small-object detection performance in dense aerial scenes.

It should be noted that the two configurations do not form a strict single-variable ablation study, since both the model scale (Nano $\rightarrow$ Small) and the input resolution ($640 \rightarrow 768$) are modified simultaneously. The purpose of this design is to investigate the practical trade-off between detection performance and computational cost under a fixed hardware resource budget.

Table~\ref{tab:yolov8_configs} summarizes the main characteristics of the two configurations.

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \caption{Comparison of the two YOLOv8 experimental configurations. GFLOPS and inference time are measured for single-image inference under the corresponding input resolution.}
    \label{tab:yolov8_configs}
    \begin{tabular}{lccc}
        \toprule
        Configuration & Parameters & GFLOPS & Inference Time \\
        \midrule
        YOLOv8n-$640$ & 3,012,798 & 8.204 & 1.309 ms \\
        YOLOv8s-$768$ & 11,139,470 & 28.666 & 2.962 ms \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

As shown in Table~\ref{tab:yolov8_configs}, YOLOv8s-$768$ significantly increases both computational complexity and inference latency compared with YOLOv8n-$640$. However, the larger model capacity and higher input resolution are expected to improve feature extraction and localization performance for small-scale objects in the VisDrone dataset.
